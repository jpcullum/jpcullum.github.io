About me

Trained as a Hydrologist at the University of Wisconsin - Stevens Point, much of my initial experience in the world of data focuses on the environmental and water worlds. Whether this was parsing through continuous gauge data or looking for statistically significant changes in precipitation over time, I began to fall in love with taking something as simple as a .txt or .csv file and telling a magnifiscent, colorful story. Once introduced to hydrologic modeling methods such as the EPA SWMM or SWAT models, I knew that I wanted to stay in the world of modeling and data. Graduating from school in the Spring of 2021, I accepted a position with the State of Kansas coordinating their Harmful Algal Bloom program. Here I honed my data analytics skill, delving into advanced R, and starting to learn SQL and Python. Currently I am looking to grow my experience in the data-world and deepen my understanding of statistics and modeling.
